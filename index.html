The World and Work of Maika D. Harrell



Software engineer, digital artist, and language arts expert, Maika Harrell works to optimize communication between people and technology.

An advocate for computer literacy, he occasionally teaches digital research practices for college students at CUNY in New York. Overall, you could say I have discovered the power and centrality of digital storytelling.

Years as an creative on the user-end of hardware and software, I've had a deep interest in computer hardware and software design since I was young. I've been a digital media creator for years, collaborating in teams to deliver video. As a graphic designer, I've produced press kits, promo videos, and pitch decks for individuals and businesses.





Experience



CUNY. College instructor, in-person and online. Designed and presented lecture-based curriculum for the English Department. Subjects included: critical thinking, argument, essay composition, and canonical literature. Taught online via Blackboard, canva, and self-made websites. Taught in person with multimedia presentations that incorporated text, photo, video, and audio sequences.

Skytown Ent. A/V Technician for local production company and actor seevices. Operated camera and audio recording equipment on set and in studio. Processed video in NLE software (ie, Adobe Premiere Pro) and exported daily deliverables. Interfaced between agents and their actor clients to schedule editing and audition sessions. Managed social media account daily.

92NY. Served as a teacher and videographer for students at the 92NY. Influenced a younger generation interested in digital media arts and digital storytelling. Taught skills and techniques concerning video equipment and media software.

Self-Employed. Editor. Freelancer of video processing and graphic design. Designed electronic press kits, online portfolios, and promo videos for clients in the entertainment industry. Edited narrative and documentary digital films. Directed teams of up to 30.

Self-Employed. Audio/Video technician.
